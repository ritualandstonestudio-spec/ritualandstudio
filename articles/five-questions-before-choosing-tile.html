The Five Questions to Ask Before You Choose a Single Tile

June 2026  ·  5 min read

Most renovation regrets trace back to the same moment: standing in a showroom, overwhelmed by the options, picking something because it looks beautiful under fluorescent lights and feels urgent to decide. The tiles arrive. The bathroom is tiled. And somewhere between grouting and final clean, you realise the space doesn't feel like what you imagined.

It almost never comes down to the tile itself. It comes down to not asking the right questions before choosing it.

These five questions won't slow you down. They'll focus you. And they'll save you from the most common, most expensive mistake in bathroom renovation.

01. What is the natural light like in this bathroom?

Light changes everything about how a tile reads in a space. A cool grey tile in a south-facing bathroom can look heavy and cold. That same tile in a north-facing room with generous windows becomes sophisticated and calm. A warm cream tile in low light looks muddy. In good light, it looks like travertine.

Before you fall in love with a sample in a showroom, take it home. Hold it in the actual room at different times of day. Morning light, afternoon light, artificial light at night. If it looks right in all three, you have your answer.

02. What is your grout maintenance tolerance?

Nobody talks about grout until they're cleaning it at 10pm on a Sunday. Grout colour, width, and sealing all directly affect how much maintenance your bathroom will require for the next decade. Large format tiles with minimal grout lines are not just a design choice. They are a lifestyle choice.

If you have young children, pets, or simply no interest in scrubbing grout, this needs to be a primary consideration, not an afterthought. A rectified tile with a 1.5mm joint sealed with an epoxy grout will behave very differently from a handmade tile with a wide, unsanded joint.

03. What feeling do you want this room to evoke?

This sounds abstract. It is the most practical question on this list.

Bathrooms are experienced with all five senses. The weight of a stone tile underfoot feels different from a glazed ceramic. A rough, honed surface reads differently in morning light than a polished one. If you want the bathroom to feel like a warm resort, you are looking at different materials than if you want it to feel like a cool, modern gallery.

Define the feeling first. Let the materials follow that feeling, not the other way around.

04. What is your actual budget per square metre?

Not your total bathroom budget. Your tile budget, per square metre, fully installed. This includes the tile itself, adhesive, grout, waterproofing membrane, and labour. A tile that costs $80/m2 retail can cost $180/m2 installed. A tile that costs $180/m2 retail on a complex layout can cost $320/m2 installed.

The showroom price is never the total price. Know your number before you start looking, not after you have fallen in love with something outside it.

05. Are these tiles going on the floor, the wall, or both?

A tile that is beautiful on a wall is not always suitable for a floor. Slip ratings, hardness ratings, and surface texture all determine where a tile can be used safely and durably. In Australia, bathroom floors require a minimum slip rating of P3 for wet areas.

Using the same tile across both floor and wall can be a beautiful, cohesive decision. It can also look monotonous if the tile does not have enough variation in its surface. Using different but complementary tiles across floor and wall is often the more considered choice, but it requires a stronger understanding of how the two will sit together.

These questions are not designed to complicate the process. They are designed to give you a clear brief before you walk into any showroom, so that you are choosing from a considered shortlist rather than reacting to whatever is in front of you.

The best tile decisions are never made in the showroom. They are made in the weeks before you get there.
