The Real Cost of a Luxury Bathroom Renovation in Australia (2026)

March 2026  ·  6 min read

Bathroom renovation budgets are set with optimism and exceeded with predictability. The gap between what homeowners expect to spend and what they actually spend is rarely the result of greed or incompetence. It is almost always the result of decisions being made too late, or not made at all, before the work begins.

This is an honest breakdown of where the money goes in a luxury bathroom renovation in Australia in 2026, and which parts of the budget you should protect at all costs.

The baseline: what a full renovation actually costs

A full bathroom renovation in a standard Australian home ranges from $18,000 to $35,000 for a mid-market outcome. A luxury renovation, with premium tiles, European tapware, custom joinery, and quality fixtures, typically sits between $35,000 and $80,000 depending on the size of the space and the complexity of the brief.

These numbers include everything: demolition, waterproofing, tiling, plumbing, electrical, fixtures, joinery, and labour. They do not include design fees, which are typically additional and, in our view, the most important part of the budget to protect.

Where the money goes

Labour: Typically 40 to 50 percent of a bathroom renovation budget. In Australia in 2026, a licensed tiler charges between $65 and $120 per hour. A plumber charges $120 to $180. An electrician runs $110 to $160. Labour is not where to save money. It is where quality is made or lost.

Waterproofing: Between $800 and $2,500 for a standard bathroom. This is the most consequential line item in any bathroom renovation and the one most often underestimated. A failed waterproofing membrane does not show itself for years. When it does, the damage is catastrophic. Do not let anyone do it cheaply.

Tiles and installation: A luxury tile selection at $80 to $250 per square metre, installed, will account for $8,000 to $25,000 of a full bathroom renovation. Large format tiles cost more to install because of the time, adhesive, and skill required to lay them properly.

Fixtures and tapware: A quality vanity, basin, shower screen, and tapware package from a European or premium Australian supplier runs $6,000 to $20,000 retail. Do not buy fixtures before your design direction is confirmed. Changing your mind after purchase rarely results in a full refund.

Where budgets blow out

Variations. Every change made after the contract is signed attracts an additional cost. A tile substitution mid-build can cost $500 to $2,000 in labour alone, depending on how far the work has progressed. A layout change after waterproofing has been done is a significant cost. These are not builder errors. They are the cost of indecision.

The second most common budget blow-out is fixtures that do not fit. A vanity ordered before the layout is finalised. Tapware that does not work with the plumbing configuration. A shower screen that does not fit the opening. All of these are avoidable with a clear design brief prepared before any products are ordered.

The one thing most budgets do not include but should

Design guidance before the build begins. A bathroom direction session or a full design plan costs a fraction of a single variation. It eliminates the decisions that are most expensive to get wrong and gives you a clear brief to take to any builder or supplier. In our experience, clients who invest in design direction before their renovation spend less overall, not more.

A luxury bathroom renovation is one of the highest-value investments you can make in your home. The way to protect that investment is not to spend more. It is to decide more carefully, earlier, before a single tile is ordered.
